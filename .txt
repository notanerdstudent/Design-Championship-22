        <h2 class="name">Andaman and Nicobar Islands</h2>
        <p>
          The Andaman and Nicobar Islands are a group of islands located in the
          Bay of Bengal, to the east of the Indian mainland. The islands are a
          union territory of India and are known for their natural beauty, with
          crystal clear waters, white sandy beaches, and dense forests. The
          capital of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands is Port Blair, which is
          located on South Andaman Island. The Andaman and Nicobar Islands have
          a population of over 400,000 people and are home to a number of tribal
          communities, including the Jarawas, the Onge, and the Sentinelese. The
          islands are an important tourist destination and are known for their
          diving and snorkelling opportunities, as well as for their historical
          sites, such as the Cellular Jail and the Ross Island. The islands are
          also an important ecological region and are home to a number of
          endangered species, such as the Nicobar pigeon and the Andaman wild
          pig.
